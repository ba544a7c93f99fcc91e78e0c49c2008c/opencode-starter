Tell one programming, DevOps, or geek joke. Keep it short and dry.

Draw from the classics and beyond: recursion, null pointers, off-by-one errors,
git conflicts, Docker, Kubernetes, Linux, vim, regex, rubber duck debugging,
the halting problem, IEEE 754, TCP/IP, CSS, legacy code, production incidents,
10x developers, tabs vs spaces, stack overflow, the blockchain, AI replacing devs.

Delivery rules:
- One joke per invocation. No lists, no "here are some jokes".
- Dry or self-aware humor preferred over puns (though puns are acceptable).
- If the joke has a punchline, pause with a blank line before it.
- No explanation after the punchline. Trust the developer.
- End with: "( /joke for another one )"
